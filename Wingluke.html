In It to Pin It!

A museum customer return incentive program, that involved researching and discovery a customer base, designing a guide to creating pins, and creating a display case.

This was a capstone project for my major at university. Myself and the team wanted to work with an actual organization in order to get experience working with a stakeholder so we reached out to the Wing Luke Museum in Seattle. We reached out to this museum in particular because we noticed there was a growing dislike toward the AAPI community during the pandemic and wanted to help out that community.

Reaching out

When we reached out to Wing Luke to ask them if there was anything they would like us to design for them, they didn't really have an idea of what we could improve through design so we did some research to discover pain points and opportunities at Wing Luke.

Creating Personas

Through data gathered from surveys, interviews with staff, and observational studies at Wing Luke I was able to create a profile of what type of people would visit the Wing Luke Museum, and used that data to generate personas.

Creating a competitive analysis

I spent some time research local museums in the Seattle, area as well as considering other businesses with a consistently returning customer base to see how they generated customer, retention, and what the Wing Luke did different from these businesses.

Ideating with user scenarios around oppertunities and pain points

I used these personas to generate user scenarios which targeted particular opportunities or alleviated pain points myself and the team noticed at the Wing Luke Museum.

Myself and the team then presented these potential solutions to the Wing Luke Museum who then wanted us to particularly focus on customer retention.

After discussing the needs of the stakeholder we decided to iterate on designs that could incentivize customer return and we came to a consensus with Wing Luke on a collectable pin program, since they are a successful incentive seen in other businesses such as PAX's Pinny Arcade, and Disney Park's Pin Collection and Trading program, and It would be within Wing Luke's budget to implement.

We then iterated on different experiences in which customers received pins, and presented those to Wing Luke. They decided they would go with my idea of a pin collection card cards.

I then created a design guide so that AAPI (Asian American, Pacific Islander) artists could created artwork for both pins and the collection cards.

Testing design guide and creating prototypes

We then had the AAPI artists on the create collection card art, and pins that we created soft prototypes for at Wing Luke.

Prototyping display cases

I created 2D and 3D prototypes for a display case for the Wing Luke Museum, that would creatively display pins to customers.
